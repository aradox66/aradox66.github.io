    The mathematics underlying inferential statistics derive from probability theory.

    Probability of $A$
    Number of outcomes in $ A = \left|A\right| $ = Cardinality of $A$
    Number of outcomes in $ S = \left|S\right| $ = Cardinality of $S$
    $P(A)=\frac{\left|A\right|}{\left|S\right|}$

    Absolute and Normalized Measurement for Model Regularization
    $\left|A\right| \subseteq \ell_{1}\text{-norm}$ measurement.
    Normalized values $ \subseteq \ell_{k}\text{-norm}$ measurements.

    Two events are mutually exclusive (disjoint) if $AB=\emptyset$.
    Two events are dependent if $ P(A) \neq P(A|B) $.
    Two events are independent if $ P(A) = P(A|B) = P(A|B^{c}) $.

    Multiplication rule for independent events
    $ P(A) = P(A|B) \implies P(AB) = P(A) * P(B) $
    The probability of $A \cap B$ is equal to the product of $P(A)$ and $P(B)$.

    $P(A \cup B) = P(A) + P(B) - P(AB)$
    $P(A \cup B \cup C) = P(A) + P(B) + P(C) - P(AB) - P(BC) - P(AC) + P(ABC)$

    $P(\bigcup{A_{i}})=\displaystyle\sum{\displaystyle\bigcap^{\text{odd}}} A_{i}-\displaystyle\sum{\displaystyle\bigcap^{\text{even}}} A_{i}$

    Combinatorics and Permutations

    Cartesian/Cross Product
    $ \left| P \times S \right| = \left| P \right| \times \left| S \right| $
    {(i, j, k, ...) for i in a for j in b for k in c ...}

    Permutations are ordered selections.
    Partial selection (number of possible permutations): $nPr = \frac{n!}{(n-r)!}$
    len(list(itertools.permutations(S, r)))

    Combinations are unordered selections.
    $ nCr = n \text{ choose } r = C_{r}^{n} = \left( _{r}^{n} \right) = \frac{n!}{(n-r)!r!}$
    len(list(itertools.combinations(S, r)))
